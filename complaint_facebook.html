Summary of Incident

In or around 2008, while the complainant was a minor aged 8 and attending primary school in Botswana, security personnel acting outside their lawful remit — and apparently on instruction from an unauthorised third party — subjected her and her family to deliberate interference and intimidation, causing significant fear and lasting psychological distress.

Grounds of Complaint
  1. Abuse of Authority – Personnel acted outside the scope of their lawful authority on instruction from an unauthorised party.
  2. Unlawful Interference – Lawful activities were deliberately disrupted without legal basis or justification.
  3. Psychological Harm to a Minor – Deliberate use of fear and intimidation against an 8-year-old constitutes a serious safeguarding failure.
  4. Failure of Oversight – Relevant authorities failed to prevent or address the misconduct at the time.
Requested Outcomes
  • A formal apology acknowledging the misconduct and the harm caused to the complainant as a child.
  • A full and independent investigation into the incident, including identification of the third party responsible.
  • That this complaint be formally recorded and retained on file by the relevant authority.
